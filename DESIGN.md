# Dispensario Nuestra Senora de la Candelaria - DESIGN.md

## 1. Visual Theme & Atmosphere

- The website should feel dignified, human, and community-rooted.
- Avoid the cold, over-corporate clinic look. This is a social health project with a devotional and neighborhood identity.
- The emotional mix is: trust, calm, warmth, coastal air, and practical service.
- Layouts should feel editorial and intentional, with breathing room, layered surfaces, and a visible hierarchy.
- Visual references are structural rather than literal: warm minimalism, cream surfaces, sea-glass greens, clay accents, and restrained depth.

## 3. Typography Rules

- Use a refined serif for major headlines to communicate history, dignity, and care.
- Use a clean modern sans-serif for body text, labels, navigation, and cards.
- Headlines should not be ultra-tight or futuristic. Keep them generous and readable.
- Body copy should stay clear and direct; never over-stylize informational content such as horarios or precios.

### Type Hierarchy

| Level | Usage | Guidance |
| --- | --- | --- |
| `display` | Hero title | Serif, high contrast, 56-72px desktop |
| `h1` | Major section headings | Serif, 38-48px desktop |
| `h2` | Secondary headings | Sans or serif depending on density, 28-34px |
| `h3` | Card titles | Sans, 20-24px, semibold |
| `body` | Paragraphs | Sans, 16-18px |
| `meta` | Labels, chips, eyebrow text | Sans, uppercase or small caps, wide tracking |

## 4. Component Stylings

### Buttons

- Primary buttons use `candelaria` background with white text.
- Secondary buttons use transparent or `surface` fills with dark borders.
- Buttons should feel solid and helpful, not playful.
- Rounded corners: 9999px for CTAs, 20-24px for cards.

### Cards

- Cards use warm light surfaces with soft borders and low, diffuse shadows.
- Each specialty card should feel informative first and decorative second.
- Use subtle top accents or chips instead of loud gradients.

### Navigation

- The header should be compact, legible, and semi-transparent over the hero.
- Navigation links remain understated until hover, then gain contrast or underline motion.

### Contact Elements

- WhatsApp actions must be visually obvious.
- Floating action button stays fixed in the bottom-right corner and uses the primary brand color.
- Phone, Instagram, and schedule blocks should look trustworthy and scannable.

## 5. Layout Principles

- Use alternating section densities: expansive hero, tighter info band, comfortable card grid, calm footer.
- Prefer asymmetry with balance. A clean split hero works better than a centered generic landing page.
- Use wide content on desktop with compact readable columns for detailed schedules.
- Let backgrounds change by section: cream, mist, layered radial gradients, and dark footer.

## 6. Depth & Elevation

- Shadows should be soft and wide, never sharp black drops.
- Use 1-2 surface levels only. The site should feel grounded, not dashboard-like.
- Decorative blur fields and radial washes are welcome when they help create atmosphere.

## 7. Do's and Don'ts

### Do

- Keep pricing transparent and easy to find.
- Make contact actions immediate.
- Highlight the community mission and Caraballeda identity.
- Use spacing to separate dense medical information.
- Preserve a sense of warmth and seriousness at the same time.

### Don't

- Do not use generic hospital blue gradients.
- Do not overload the page with icons, badges, or stock-dashboard patterns.
- Do not use glossy glassmorphism or neon accents.
- Do not bury horarios inside overly decorative cards.
- Do not make the site feel like a startup SaaS template.

## 8. Responsive Behavior

- On mobile, the hero stacks into a single column with CTA buttons near the top.
- Specialty cards collapse into a single column with generous touch spacing.
- The floating WhatsApp button must stay reachable without covering key text.
- Tables should become stacked pricing cards on small screens.

## 9. Agent Prompt Guide

- If adding new sections, keep the same cream-and-teal editorial language.
- New UI should feel civic, local, and grounded in service.
- When in doubt, choose clarity over ornament.
- Use warm neutrals, teal anchors, and one clay accent for emphasis.
